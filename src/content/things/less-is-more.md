---
title: building with people
date: march 2026
---

i need people to build things. not because i lack skills, but because i lose momentum. a project without someone to bounce ideas off, to argue about button colors with - it just dies in my projects graveyard.

but here's the part nobody talks about: new people terrify me.

not in a "networking is scary" way. in a _what happens to my existing people_ way.

i have friends from university back in ukraine. we're scattered now - ukraine, canada, the US. we talk every week. i've been dreaming about building something together since we graduated. one of them and i are working on a SaaS product right now. but he has a full-time job plus side projects, so sometimes i'm waiting days for a reply. another friend and i keep looking for even a small project to collaborate on. the will is there. the time just isn't.

then completely by accident i met two people, we started talking, and a month ago we incorporated an actual company. they're great. we move fast. it works.

and i felt guilty. like i was replacing my friends by building with someone new. like there's a finite amount of "building together" energy and i was spending it on strangers.

that's irrational. working with new people doesn't erase years of friendship. but the fear is real, and i think a lot of people - especially immigrants who already lost proximity to everyone they love - carry it quietly.

i don't look for collaborators. i don't network strategically. people just show up, or old friends finally have a free weekend. and somehow, things get built.

still figuring out how to make room for new people without feeling like i'm betraying the old ones
