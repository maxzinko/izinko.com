---
title: good enough
date: march 2026
---

here's something i've noticed - people stopped asking "was this made with AI?"

not because they can't tell. but because they _don't care anymore_. if the thing works, looks right, and solves their problem, the tool behind it is irrelevant. nobody asks what camera a good photo was taken with. okay, some people do. but those people are annoying.

manual-everything workflows are becoming a flex, not a standard. spending 6 hours hand-crafting something AI could scaffold in 10 minutes isn't dedication - it's stubbornness. the craft isn't in refusing the tool, it's in knowing what to do with the output. _editing, taste, judgment_ - that's the actual skill now.

the people who win aren't the ones avoiding AI. they're the ones using it fast enough that no one thinks to ask.

we crossed the "good enough" line a while ago. most people just haven't admitted it yet.
