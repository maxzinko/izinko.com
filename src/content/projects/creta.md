a friend of mine runs an outdoor clothing brand in ukraine. products named after carpathian mountains, made by hand in ivano-frankivsk. his old site was a no-code landing page with no real store. no product pages, no checkout, no backend. just vibes.

so i built him a real one. [next.js](https://nextjs.org) frontend, [sanity](https://sanity.io) for content, [mono](https://monobank.ua) for payments, [nova poshta](https://novaposhta.ua) API for delivery addresses, [resend](https://resend.com) for emails, and a [telegram](https://telegram.org) bot that pings him when orders come in.

no strict requirements. just "make it look good and actually work." three months of back and forth - figuring out what he needed by building it, showing it, adjusting, repeating. the result is a full e-commerce platform he controls completely. products, collections, pricing, content - all his to manage.

if i started over i'd probably grab a boilerplate and restyle it. but there's something about building from zero that forces you to understand every piece. i'm proud of this one. maybe not 100% done - _100 is impossible_ - but close enough to ship.
